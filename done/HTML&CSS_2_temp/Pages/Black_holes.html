What are black holes

A black hole is a point in space time where nothing not even a single particle or light beam can escape. There is a ring around black holes called the bouncy of no escape you can also call it the event horizon, this ring has this name because anything that crosses this ring has to be traveling faster than the speed of light to escape (for as far as we currently know this is not possible). A black hole acts a lot like a black body since it does not reflect any light at all. There are theories that predict that the event horizons emit called Hawking radiation. Every black hole has a singularly inside it this is a single point where all the mass is located. We don’t exactly know what it. A singularly may be infinitely dense meaning that all the mass is located in a single point in space with no surface or volume, or something completely different right now we don’t know its like trying to decide by 0. A common mistake about black holes is that they don’t “suck” anything in, so if we where to replace the sun with a equally big black hole nothing would chance (except the temperature).
